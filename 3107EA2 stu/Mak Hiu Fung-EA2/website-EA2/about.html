G.E.M.

G.E.M. (Gloria Tang Tsz Kei), born on August 16, 1991, is a Hong Kong singer-songwriter known for her bold and innovative style. She debuted at the age of 16 on July 10, 2008, winning the New Artist Award at Hong Kong's top music award shows. In 2012, her album Xposed made her the highest-selling female artist in Hong Kong, and she became the youngest nominee for Best Female Singer at the 24th Golden Melody Awards in 2013.

G.E.M. gained rapid fame in the Chinese-speaking world after her standout performances on Hunan TV's singing competition I Am a Singer in 2014. By the age of 24, she had completed 80 solo concerts, with her August 2015 concert at Beijing Workers' Stadium attracting nearly 40,000 attendees. In November 2015, she performed her 73rd and final show of the X.X.X. Live world tour at Wembley Arena in London.

In May 2017, she celebrated her 100th concert during the Queen of Hearts world tour in Wuhan. Her song "Light Years Away" reached over 100 million views on YouTube in January 2018, and by January 2023, it surpassed 260 million views, making it the most-watched Mandarin song on the platform. Other hits like "Goodbye," "Countdown," and "Devil from Heaven" also exceeded 100 million views between 2019 and 2021. In 2023, her song "I Like You" joined the ranks, making her the first Mandarin female artist with five music videos surpassing 100 million views. As of January 2023, G.E.M.'s YouTube channel has accumulated over 2 billion total views.
